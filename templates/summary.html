By the numbers

Schools at a Glance

There a variety of schools serving various purposes in the US and its territories. At a high-level, what is going on with the schools? What's their distributions look like? Would someone know whether schools are generally expensive or cheap? Questions like these require an initial understanding of the data, and hopefully we can provide some of that insight here.

There are some interesting variables to comb over in the college data, which lead to interesting conclusions. Some of the facts about the data might even blow your mind!
  • Did you know that there over 7000 accredited institutions in the US and its territories?
  • A variety of programs are offered - in fact, well over 35 major programs are offered across the institutions.
  • Over 50% of students get Pell grants when applying for colleges - makes the financial aid easier to get!
School Type

There are three primary school types - Public, Private, and Private for Profit. Public schools are open to all students and funded through local, state, and federal taxes. Private schools are those that are funded primarily through tuition and grants, but don't operate as profit-institutions. Private for profit are schools that tend to serve specific purposes, such as additional professional training and vocational education.

School Enrollment

While there are three different types of schools, the enrollment of each time is far from equal. Here, we can see that there is a heavy skew towards public schools, almost three times that of the next highest enrollment type, Private. Why is that?

Perhaps one reason might be that public schools are more affordable than private schools. Hovering over each type shows how much each school type costs, which can show some clear results.

Degrees Awarded

Due to the nature of each type of school, they tend to server different crowds of students. Public schools allow for studies in many fields, and often present opportunities in further study after graduation. Private schools can leverage the value of their brand, and students may only rely on a bachelors for enhancing their career prospects. Private for profit schools are generally not extensive academic institutions, providing only the knowledge needed to advance down a specific career path.
